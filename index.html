About me

I am a research engineer and innovator with a Ph.D. in Computer Graphics from the University of California, Santa Barbara, where I was mentored by Professor Ted Kim. My expertise lies in computational graphics, simulation, and optimization, with a focus on developing scalable solutions that address industry challenges.

As the main inventor to SSP (Spatial Simulation and Packing) technology, I have led its development and commercialization across diverse industries such as 3D printing, robotics, and manufacturing. My work has been successfully deployed to our clients to optimize their workflows and reduce costs. One highlight of my career is the publication "Dense, Interlocking-Free and Scalable Spectral Packing of Generic 3D Objects", presented at SIGGRAPH 2023 and widely covered by major outlets such as MIT News, Yahoo, and Voxelmatters.

My research spans physically based animation, geometry processing, and shape analysis, where I have developed efficient spectral methods for 3D packing and smoke simulation.

Beyond research, I contribute to education as a Software Development Fellow at the Open Avenues Foundation, where I design and teach courses on computer graphics, focusing on WebGL and Three.js. Additionally, I mentor students, guiding them in career development and technical excellence.

Commercialization and Technology Transfer

I spearhead the research and development of innovative software products based on the SSP technology I invented. These advancements have successfully been commercialized into the following products:

Inkbit Construct This is Inkbit's proprietary build preparation tool leverages SSP to streamline 3D printing operations. By automating part arrangement, it significantly reduces manual labor, improving build preparation efficiency and boosting printer throughput by up to 30%.

Fitting Pack Developed for a leading candy and toy manufacturer, this tool ensures toys fit into designated containers while incorporating advanced digital safety checks. By replacing manual processes with digital automation, it revolutionized the client's design workflow, achieving substantial time and cost savings.

Flex Pack Designed for IMA , a global leader in box-on-demand machines, this software selects optimal box sizes for cutting machines, reducing shipping costs. Additionally, it analyzes client order histories to optimize box dimensions further, achieving even greater savings.

SSP in Synera In collaboration with Synera, a leader in industrial automation software, we integrated SSP into the Synera marketplace. This ongoing project aims to make build preparation for additive manufacturing more accessible and cost-effective for a broader range of companies.

SSP for Robotics Packing This evaluation project was conducted in collaboration with a major e-commerce company. We integrated SSP into Nvidia Isaac Sim to develop a robotic box-packing simulation. During the evaluation, our solution outperformed the company's existing system, demonstrating superior efficiency and accuracy.

These products have generated significant additional revenue for Inkbit while delivering transformative solutions for our partners and clients. Please visit https://inkbit3d.com/packing/ for more details.
